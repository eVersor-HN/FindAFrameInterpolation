FindAFrameInterpolation

The free Windows video player that makes 24 fps look like 120.

FAFI plays your videos with real-time motion interpolation — a GPU MEMC engine or a neural RIFE engine turns 24/30/60 fps material into buttery-smooth playback at your monitor's refresh rate. Feed it music instead and it becomes a full-screen visualiser that reacts to every beat. Offline-first: no ads, no account, no telemetry.

Download FAFI-Setup.exe GitHub
Free for personal & commercial use · Windows 10/11 (64-bit) · Direct3D-11 GPU (RIFE needs Vulkan)

Smooth motion, two engines

Films are 24 fps; monitors are 60–240 Hz. FAFI generates the frames in between in real time — what TV makers call motion smoothing, but on your PC, under your control, switchable live while the video plays.

MEMC — fast & light

A fast, lightweight motion engine that's ready the instant you hit play. Runs on essentially any modern GPU.

RIFE — neural quality

A neural engine for the cleanest interpolation on hard motion, switchable with one key (E). Works out of the box — nothing extra to download.

GPU decode

Hardware-accelerated decoding on your GPU — H.264, HEVC, VP9, AV1 and virtually every other format and container.

Feed it music

Open an audio file and FAFI stops pretending to be a video player: the whole screen turns into a reactive visualiser that moves to every beat, edge to edge, at your monitor's refresh. Hide the controls and it's just you, the track and the light.

Reactive, not decorative

A whole gallery of live visuals that actually react — hard, and on the beat. None of that "wiggles a bit if you squint" filler.

Flick or shuffle

Switch visuals with the arrow keys, or let shuffle deal you a fresh one on a timer. Full-screen, sharp, nothing in the way.

Still a player

Drop a video in at any moment — open a file, paste a URL, drag it on — and FAFI snaps straight back to picture. Your eyes and ears take turns; you don't have to choose.

A complete player, not a demo

  • In-player ambient light — a high-quality glow of the picture's own colours spills into the black around the video, lighting up its own frame with no LED strip or extra hardware. Tune the reach, softness, brightness, colour, sides and picture size; it composes with every retro look and takes on their colour.
  • Retro screen looks — optional CRT, Trinitron, LCD, NTSC, 35 mm film, VHS, old handheld, E-Ink, Technicolor and more, each with adjustable strength, plus a curved-screen toggle.
  • Upscaling — high-quality upscaling with halo-free sharpening, optional internal 4K render path.
  • Subtitles — SRT/ASS/SSA/VTT with full ASS styling, embedded tracks, auto-fetched platform subtitles, live timing correction — rendered on a sharp layer that is never interpolated.
  • Accessibility — a built-in toolkit: colour-blind assist, dialogue boost / mono / amplify, a flash guard and calmer UI for photosensitivity, and pitch-preserving slow-motion.
  • Picture & sound — image filter presets, a 10-band equalizer, virtual surround, correct 5.1/7.1 mapping, smart loudness, per-ear balance and pitch-preserving tempo.
  • Couch-ready menu — every control lives in a honeycomb menu you open with a right-click or the Escape key, and drive entirely from the keyboard: arrows glide between tiles, Space picks, Escape backs out. No mouse, no leaving the sofa.
  • Streaming as an extra — paste a web video URL and it plays smooth: download-while-playing, skip to any point instantly (forward or back, no re-loading), robust against throttling, completely ad-free. Local playback needs no network at all.
  • Export — render the interpolated, filtered result to a file, subtitles as a real track or burned in.
  • Clean UI — a slim auto-hiding seekbar; the chrome fades away when idle, and fullscreen is truly borderless.

Private by design

FAFI is built as an offline player for your own files — it works with the network cable pulled. No telemetry, no phone-home, no account. The only network activity is the streaming you explicitly start.
